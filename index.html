Your first shipment roadmap
  1. Validate buyer & choose Incoterms.
  2. Identify HS code (Nigeria ➜ US HTS).
  3. Check US agency rules (FDA/USDA/EPA, etc.).
  4. Confirm tariff rate & any exclusions/programs.
  5. Book freight & insure cargo.
  6. Prepare invoice, packing list, certificates.
  7. US broker files entry; deliver to buyer.

Note: Regulations change. Always verify with official sources and your broker.

Export checklist
  • Confirm US buyer/importer of record (IOR) and FSVP responsibilities for foods.
  • Determine HS code and preferential program eligibility.
  • Get necessary certificates: Phyto, Certificate of Origin, FDA Facility Registration (foods).
  • Agree Incoterms + payment terms (LC, CAD, OA).
  • Book freight (air/sea) + cargo insurance.
  • Prepare commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading/airway bill.
  • US entry: file with customs broker; provide docs & prior notice (if FDA foods).
  • Arrange US domestic delivery and final QA checks.

This is a starter list. Your broker and buyer may add items.

US compliance quick guide

Food & agriculture
  • FDA: Facility registration, Prior Notice (foods), labeling.
  • USDA-APHIS: Phytosanitary certificates, pest controls.
  • FSVP: US importer must verify foreign suppliers (foods).
  • Organic? Verify NOP certification equivalence.

Coordinate early with your US buyer/importer for responsibilities and documentation.

Industrial & consumer goods
  • CPSC: Consumer product safety/labeling.
  • EPA: Chemicals, pesticides, TSCA declarations.
  • FCC: Radio/Wi-Fi devices authorization.
  • Textiles: Fiber content & country of origin labels.

Regulatory scope depends on product specifics—get a compliance review before shipping.

Incoterms at a glance
EXW
Buyer picks up at seller’s facility; buyer bears most costs/risks.
FOB
Seller delivers on board at origin port; risk transfers once loaded.
CIF
Seller pays to US port incl. insurance; risk transfers at loading.
DAP
Seller arranges carriage to US place; buyer handles import/duty.
DDP
Seller responsible for US import clearance & duties (use carefully).

FAQ & resources

Common questions
How do I find the correct US duty rate?

Find your HS code, then consult the US Harmonized Tariff Schedule (HTS). Confirm if any trade programs, exclusions, or quota apply. When in doubt, ask a broker or request a binding ruling.

Do I need FDA approval to export foods?

Most foods do not need pre-approval, but facilities must register with FDA, Prior Notice must be filed before arrival, and the US importer must meet FSVP obligations. Certain products have extra rules.

Can I ship DDP to the USA?

Possible, but risky—DDP makes the seller responsible for US import clearance and taxes. Many exporters prefer DAP or CIF with the buyer as importer of record.
